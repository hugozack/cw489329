STUDIO HISTORY

Studio Ghibli was founded on June 15, 1985, the studio is headed by the directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata and the producer Toshio Suzuki. Prior to the formatio of studio, Miyazaki and Takahata had already had long careers in Japanese film and television animation. The studio was founded after the success of the 1984 film Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind.

NAMING THE STUDIO

The name Ghibli was given by Hayao Miyazaki from the Italian noun ghbli, based on the Libyan-Arabic name for the hot desert wind of the country. The idea behind it was to "blow a new wind through the anime industry". It also refers to an Italian aircraft, the Caproni Ca. 309 Ghibli. Although the Italian word is more accurately transliterated as Giburi, the Japanese name of the studio is Jiburi.
